168-hour Audit Grid. 30 pts.

IN-CLASS · 15 MIN 30

PG 2/5  ·  PAINT WHERE YOUR HOURS ACTUALLY GO

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

"I have no time" is too vague to fix. "I have 47 hours of discretionary time and 22 of them are on TikTok" is fixable.

The Audit gives you reps on the move that converts a feeling ("overwhelmed") into a number you can re-budget. Most students are off by 15–25 hours/week on the question — and the gap is the lesson.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Paint every cell of the 168-hour grid with one of 8 categories.
  • Use real data — phone screen-time, calendar, paystub. Not vibes.
  • Total each category. Compare to your guess from minute 1.
  • Mark one leak — the category you'd take back. That's where Sunday's re-budget starts.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 30 points total.

Pass line: 19 pts. Below 19 → free revision week, no penalty. We grade the honesty, not the totals.

Eisenhower Triage. 25 pts.

IN-CLASS · 20 MIN 25

PG 3/5  ·  URGENT × IMPORTANT — AND THE PERMISSION TO DELETE

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

If everything on your list is "urgent," you don't have a list — you have a panic. The Eisenhower 2×2 forces you to separate what's on fire from what's important, which are not the same thing.

The hardest skill in the module is the DELETE quadrant. Most first-years cannot put anything there. Yours has to have at least one.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Take this week's actual to-do list — not invented, not aspirational.
  • Drop every task into one of four quadrants: DO · SCHEDULE · SHRINK · DELETE.
  • Force at least one task into DELETE. (No exceptions.)
  • Add a one-line justification to each quadrant for why that bucket fits.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 25 points total.

Pass line: 16 pts. Below 16 → free revision week, no penalty. The DELETE quadrant is where points are won and lost.

Tomorrow's ABC List. 25 pts.

IN-CLASS · 12 MIN 25

PG 4/5  ·  A SINGLE PAGE YOU'LL ACTUALLY USE TOMORROW

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

The audit told you what you have. The matrix told you what matters. The list is where it stops being theory and starts being tomorrow morning.

The constraint is realism: max 3 A's, real time estimates, total < 4 hours of focused work. A list that won't fit is a wish.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Pull from your TRIAGE matrix. A's come from DO. B's from SCHEDULE. C's from anywhere.
  • Cap A's at three. Each with a realistic time estimate (in minutes).
  • Tally A column. If > 4 hrs of focus → cut. If < 1 hr → add (tomorrow's wasted).
  • Identify the "first A" — the one you'll start within 30 min of waking up.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 25 points total.

Pass line: 16 pts. Below 16 → free revision week, no penalty. We're grading whether tomorrow has a fighting chance, not whether you executed.

Time Log + Sunday Ritual. 50 pts.

TAKE-HOME · WEEKLY 50

PG 5/5  ·  THE GAP BETWEEN PLANNED AND DONE IS THE LESSON

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

One ABC list is a coin flip. A weekly Sunday Ritual + daily log over a term is a system — and the system is where the actual time literacy lives.

We trade polish for honesty. We want your real Wednesday — A's planned, A's done, what leaked. The gap is the data.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Sunday (15 min): run the four-step Loop. AUDIT (3) · MAP (3) · TRIAGE (5) · RE-BUDGET (4).
  • Each weekday: log one row — A's planned · A's done · what leaked · 1-line note.
  • End of week: 4-sentence reflection — biggest leak, biggest win, broken assumption, next Sunday's fix.
  • Bring it Mondays. First 10 minutes of class we read patterns together.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 50 points total.

Pass line: 33 pts. Below 33 → free revision week, no penalty. A messy log with real patterns beats a sparkling one with no learning.
