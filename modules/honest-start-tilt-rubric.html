Procrastination Autopsy. 40 pts.

IN-CLASS · 25 MIN 40

PG 2/4  ·  DIAGNOSE ONE MOMENT — NOT A LIFETIME

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

"I procrastinate" is too big to fix. "Tuesday at 9:47pm I avoided the Y211 lab because I didn't know what 'done' looked like" is fixable.

The Autopsy gives you reps on the move that converts a lifetime narrative into a single, addressable moment. The four triggers tell you which fix to reach for.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Pick one specific moment from the last 7 days.
  • Answer seven forensic questions — when, where, what was due, what you did instead, the trigger, what's underneath, the tiny step.
  • Match the moment to one of the four triggers (ambiguity / fear / boredom / exhaustion).
  • Prescribe a tiny step due tonight. Halve it. Halve it again.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 40 points total.

Pass line: 26 pts. Below 26 → free revision week, no penalty. We grade the diagnosis, not whether your night went well.

Motivation Stack. 40 pts.

IN-CLASS · 20 MIN 40

PG 3/4  ·  AUTONOMY · MASTERY · PURPOSE

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

Self-Determination Theory says intrinsic motivation needs three nutrients: autonomy (choice), mastery (visible progress), and purpose (something that matters).

Most rituals fail because they only feed one. Yours will feed all three — by design, on paper, before a bad week tests it.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Name three autonomy levers — where can YOU choose? Time, place, music, order.
  • Name three mastery levers — what does progress concretely look like?
  • Name three purpose levers — why does this matter to a human you love?
  • Design one 25-min ritual: when/where/what, plus which of your 9 levers it pulls.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 40 points total.

Pass line: 26 pts. Below 26 → free revision week, no penalty. The ritual will be revised again on Sunday — that's the point.

Rubric — four criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet Pts
Autonomy levers 8 PTS 7–8Three levers, all genuine choices YOU control. Specific to your life. 5–6Three levers; one is generic ("morning"). 3–4Two levers, or all generic. 0–2One or none. /8
Mastery levers 8 PTS 7–8Three concrete signals of progress — countable, visible, not vibes. 5–6Three levers; one is fuzzy ("feel productive"). 3–4Two levers, or all fuzzy. 0–2One or none. /8

Start Log. 50 pts.

TAKE-HOME · 7 DAYS 50

PG 4/4  ·  HONEST ENGAGEMENT BEATS POLISH

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

One Start Moment is a coin flip. Seven days of moments is a pattern — and the pattern is where the real diagnosis lives.

The log trades polish for honesty. We want your real Wednesday-morning row, not a curated one. The reflection is where the learning lives.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Pair with one classmate. Trade phone numbers. Different major if possible.
  • Tonight: text "DONE" when your tiny step is done. Not before.
  • Each day for 7 days, log one Start Moment row: when · trigger · tiny step · started? · one-line surprise.
  • Sunday: write a 4-sentence reflection — pattern, surprise, breakdown, next move.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 50 points total.

Pass line: 33 pts. Below 33 → free revision week, no penalty. A messy log with real patterns beats a sparkling one with no learning.

Rubric — four criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet Pts
Coverage 15 PTS 13–157 days logged. ≥ 7 rows. Real distribution — not all entered Sunday night. 10–126–7 days. 5–7 rows. Mostly real-time. 6–94–5 days. Or 7 days but very thin. 0–5Three days or fewer. Or invented retroactively. /15
Trigger tagging 10 PTS 9–10Each row tagged with primary trigger. Mix is plausible — not all four every time. 7–8Most rows tagged; 1–2 missing. Mix plausible. 4–6Some rows tagged. Many marked uniformly. 0–3Tags absent or all blank. /10
Honesty signal 10 PTS 9–10"Started?" column shows real variety (yes / no / kinda). Surprises name something specific — wins and failures. 7–8Some variety. Surprises mostly substantive. 4–6Almost all "yes." Surprises generic ("learned a lot"). 0–3Reads like fan-fiction. Suspiciously clean. /10

Next move vague. 0–5Missing or under 2 sentences. /15
