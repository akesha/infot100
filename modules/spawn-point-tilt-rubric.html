Capability Map. 30 pts.

IN-CLASS · 20 MIN 30

PG 2/4  ·  EIGHT TOOLS — EIGHT CONCRETE IDEAS

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

You can't combine capabilities you don't know. The Capability Map forces you to think in verbs — cut, print, stitch, press, cast, protect, capture, design — not in tool names.

By the end of this you have a personal map of which 2–3 capabilities are the spine of your final project. We refer back to this every week through Week 14.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • For each of the 8 Creator Commons tools, write a concrete idea for YOUR final project.
  • Each idea names a real object, a real user, and a real problem.
  • Rate each: must / maybe / no. "Must" means you'll actually use it in 14 weeks.
  • Auto-saves in the browser. You'll bring this to Week 4.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 30 points total.

Pass line: 20 pts. Below 20 → free revision week, no penalty. We grade specificity — not the cleverness of your ideas.

Cardboard Prototype. 40 pts.

IN-CLASS · 30 MIN 40

PG 3/4  ·  PROBLEM · SHAPE · BUILD · NOTICE

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

The whole semester is the loop problem → shape → make → notice → iterate. We run it in 30 minutes today, in cardboard, on a tiny problem, so your hands know the move before the stakes get high.

Most students freeze in Week 4 because Week 1 was their first time committing a shape. Not in this class.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Pick one real, small problem you had this week. Specific.
  • Sketch it (paper or Tinkercad). Don't perfect — commit a shape.
  • Build it. Cardboard, tape, hot glue. ChompSaw or Cameo if needed.
  • Photograph it + write 3 lines: worked / broke / next.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 40 points total.

Pass line: 26 pts. Below 26 → free revision week, no penalty. Polish costs you points here. Pretty cardboard means you stopped iterating.

Project Seed. 30 pts.

IN-CLASS · 10 MIN 30

PG 4/4  ·  A HYPOTHESIS, NOT A CONTRACT

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

By Week 4 you'll have a working v0.1. By Week 14 you'll ship. None of that happens if Week 1 ends without a shape for the project.

The seed is a hypothesis you can argue with. We expect it to change — we just need it concrete enough to test against reality.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • WHO is it for? A specific person or group.
  • WHAT is their problem? The pain, not the solution.
  • WHAT will you make? A physical thing.
  • WHICH 2–3 capabilities will you combine?
  • SMALLEST version you could ship by Week 4?
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Five criteria, four levels, 30 points total.

Pass line: 20 pts. Below 20 → free revision week, no penalty. An honestly rough seed beats a polished pitch deck.

Rubric — five criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet Pts
WHO is specific 6 PTS 5–6One specific person or sub-group. "Freshmen in Briscoe with one shared bathroom" beats "students." 4Specific group, but slightly broad ("CS freshmen"). 2–3"Students." "People." "Anyone." 0–1Missing or "everyone." /6
Problem is real 6 PTS 5–6Names a real pain point with a verb ("can't find," "keeps losing"). Not the solution — the pain. 4Real problem, slightly generic. 2–3Reads more like a feature wish than a pain. 0–1Missing, or solution disguised as problem. /6

WHY THIS RUBRIC LOOKS THIS WAY Notice no axis rewards "good idea" or "original concept." Originality emerges from iteration — you can't grade it Week 1. We grade specificity: who, what, which verbs, smallest version. The seed will be wrong. The skill is having one concrete enough to argue with.
