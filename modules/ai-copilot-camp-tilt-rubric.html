Prompt Lab. 35 pts.

IN-CLASS · 25 MIN 35

PG 2/3  ·  ENGINEER YOUR OWN CO-PILOT PROMPT

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

You will use AI in college. Then in jobs. The question is not whether — it's whether you'll use it like Devon (dependent) or like Robin (co-pilot).

This lab gives you reps on the move that separates them: writing a prompt that frames the problem, scopes the ask, and stays honest about who did what.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • Pick a real assignment from any class, due in the next 2 weeks.
  • Write three progressive prompts — V1 Vague, V2 with FRAME, V3 full Loop.
  • Sign the Credit Ledger — for 10 sub-tasks, mark ME / SHARED / AI.
  • Submit on Canvas before pair audit. Saves to your browser as you type.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Five criteria, four levels, 35 points total.

Pass line: 24 pts. Below 24 → free revision week, no penalty. We want you to land the move, not lose points on it.

Rubric — five criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet Pts

AI CO-PILOT CAMP · TILT RUBRIC · PG 2 / 3 v2.0 · 2026

Prompt Journal. 50 pts.

TAKE-HOME · 5 DAYS 50

PG 3/3  ·  HONEST ENGAGEMENT BEATS POLISH

① PURPOSE

The skill we're building.

One prompt is a moment. Five days of prompts is a pattern — and patterns are where you find the parts of your AI use that need to change.

This journal trades polish for honesty. We want to see your real Tuesday-night ask, not a curated version. The reflection is where the learning lives.

② TASK

What you'll do.

  • For 5 consecutive days, log every AI ask you make.
  • ~30 sec per row: When · Class & ask · Loop hit (F/P/V/O) · Owner (ME/SHARED/AI) · Surprise.
  • On Friday, write a 4-sentence reflection covering pattern, surprise, breakdown, and next move.
  • Submit on Canvas. Markdown template provided. Bring a copy to next class.
③ CRITERIA

What "got it" looks like.

See the rubric below. Four criteria, four levels, 50 points total.

Pass line: 33 pts. Below 33 → free revision week, no penalty. Honest engagement beats polish. A messy journal with real patterns beats a sparkling one with no learning.

Rubric — four criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet Pts
Coverage 15 PTS 13–155 days logged. ≥ 8 rows total. Realistic distribution across classes. 10–125 days logged. 5–7 rows. Mostly one class. 6–93–4 days. Or 5 days but very thin. 0–5Two days or fewer. Or invented retroactively. /15
Loop tagging 10 PTS 9–10Each row tagged with which Loop step(s) you ran. Distribution looks plausible — not all four every time. 7–8Most rows tagged; 1–2 missing. Mix is plausible. 4–6Some rows tagged. Many marked "all four" implausibly. 0–3Tags absent or all blank. /10
Honesty signal 10 PTS 9–10Owner column shows real variety. Surprises name something you didn't expect — wins and failures. 7–8Some variety in Owner. Surprises mostly substantive. 4–6Owner uniform. Surprises generic ("learned a lot"). 0–3Reads like fan-fiction. Suspiciously clean. /10
Reflection 15 PTS 13–154 sentences. Pattern is specific, surprise is honest, breakdown names a real Loop failure, next move is one concrete behavior change. 10–124 sentences. 3 of 4 land; one is generic. 6–9Reflection present but mostly summary. Next move vague. 0–5Missing or under 2 sentences. /15
