FINAL DEMO · 01 / 06v2.0 · 2026
CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 08 · WHY THIS LESSON

Why now, why this way.

Most "final demos" are last-day theater — students rehearse a polished pitch, deliver it once, then forget about it. Final Demo + Reckoning is built differently. The demo is layered (three rounds × increasing audience size) so the v1 gets stress-tested live. The reckoning anchors the experience to the 5 Big Ideas the whole course was built on, so students leave with portable lessons, not just a closed-out grade.

The trajectory closes the loop. The Week-1 cold-open (a Week-14 student looking back) is now actually you, talking to the next cohort's W1. That framing matters — the strongest demos here become the recordings that shape next year's onboarding.

§ A Outcomes — what every student leaves with

  • A v1 demo given live to at least 3 classmates and a TA. The TA records 30 seconds of the strongest moment.
  • A 5 Big Ideas reckoning — five lines, one per Big Idea, referencing their own vault by note name.
  • A trajectory paragraph — what they're going to ship next and what habit from this course gets them there.
  • A final Export-your-work .txt dropped into Canvas. The .txt is the entire course's record of the student, externalized.

§ B The 5 Big Ideas (reference for the reckoning)

#1
Making is iteration, not inspiration.
#2
Verbs over tools — capabilities precede equipment.
#3
Time is a system you instrument, not a virtue.
#4
Knowledge worth keeping must be externalized.
#5
Tools — including AI — are collaborators to interrogate.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 08 · PRE-CLASS · 24 HOURS OUT

Pre-class checklist.

Final Demo runs on stagecraft. Hard stops, audience structure, and recording cadence are the load-bearing details.

§ D Room + tech

  • Room re-arrangement: studio mode for rounds 1–2, open-studio circulation for round 3. Confirm desks move.
  • Hard-stop signal: lights off-on, a bell, or a music change. Test it.
  • Each TA gets a phone or recorder. Aim for 30 sec per student of strongest demo moment.
  • Visible timer for each round (15 min · 20 min · 15 min).
  • Background music for round 3 only — open-studio energy needs ambient noise.

§ E Demo prep · the morning of

  • Slack/Discord post: "Final demo is today. Bring v1 — whatever shape it's in. Bring your vault on your laptop."
  • Confirm with TAs the recording assignments. Each TA covers 5–8 students. Each demo gets 30 sec captured.
  • Optional: invite faculty observers for round 3 only. Round 1–2 needs cohort intimacy. Round 3 can take outsiders.
  • Prepare the "trust dial" prompt — the one from W6 AI Co-Pilot Camp. Students will re-rate it during the reckoning.

§ F Materials

  • Slides + speaker notes (`final-demo-slides.html`)
  • TILT rubric (`final-demo-tilt-rubric.html`)
  • 5 Big Ideas reference card (printed, 1-pager, on every desk)
  • Trajectory worksheet (1-pager, printed — backup if vaults are flaky)
  • Hard-stop signal (bell or playlist)
  • TA recording phones/recorders + storage location for clips

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 08 · RUN OF SHOW · §§ 00–02

Run of show. First half.

§ 00 Roll call · what's in your vault 10 MIN · OBSIDIAN OPEN · COUNT
+0OPEN — count the externalization
Eight weeks ago you didn't have a vault. Today, you do. Open Obsidian. Count the notes in Weeks/. Count the bug logs. Count the sources. Count the reflections. Whatever the numbers are, those are real artifacts that you produced. The vault IS the warm-up — the externalization made visible.

Do

  • Students open Obsidian. They count notes in each folder. They write the four numbers on the trajectory worksheet (or in their vault).
  • Spot-check three students aloud — read off their numbers. The point is not who has the most. The point is that the numbers exist at all.

Watch for

The student whose vault is mostly empty. They probably skipped the capture templates. Don't shame — sit with them, ask "what would you keep from the last 7 weeks if you had to pick three things?" Have them write three notes now. Better late than never.
§ 01 Cold open · the Week-1 student is now you 5 MIN · CLOSE THE LOOP
+10FRAME — bookend to Spawn Point

Do

  • Slides 1–2: replay (or read aloud) the W1 cold open. The "Week-14 student in the back row" frame.
  • One question: "What would you tell the Week-1 you, sitting in this room eight weeks ago?" Wait. Let the silence work.
  • Hand to TAs: "We have three demo rounds in the next 50 minutes. Get ready to talk about what you made."
§ 02 Demo round 1 · pairs 15 MIN · 1:1 · LOWEST STAKES
+15SHOW — partner-to-partner, 5 min each direction

Do

  • Pair up. Partner A demos for 5 min. Partner B asks one question, gives one observation. Switch.
  • Each demo narrates the trajectory in three sentences: seed (W1) → v0.1 (W4) → v1 (today).
  • TAs roam, recording 30 sec of each demo's strongest moment. Students don't need to know which moment.

Watch for

The student whose demo lives entirely on a slide deck. The constraint is "show the artifact." Slides are not the artifact. Open the actual code, the actual prototype, the actual document. If their v1 is broken, they show the broken state — that's still the artifact.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 08 · RUN OF SHOW · §§ 03–06

Run of show. Second half.

§ 03 Demo round 2 · table of 4 20 MIN · 4 STUDENTS · 3 MIN EACH
+30SCALE — same demo, bigger audience

Do

  • Form tables of 4. Each student gets 3 min: 2 min demo, 1 min Q&A.
  • Two observations from the table get written into each student's Bugs/ folder. Tables rotate the role of "scribe."
  • Hard-stop signal at 3 min. Use it. Without it, the round leaks 6 minutes per table and dies.

Watch for

The table where one student dominates and others recede. As facilitator, sit briefly at any table where this happens. The recede pattern is usually about confidence, not capacity — direct a question at the quiet student.
§ 04 Demo round 3 · open studio 15 MIN · ANYONE-CAN-VISIT · MUSIC ON
+50WALK — circulation, not performance

Do

  • Music on. Students stand or sit at their stations. Anyone can visit anyone else's desk.
  • The strongest projects pull crowds. The quietest projects get focused attention from a TA. Both are correct outcomes.
  • If faculty observers are present, this is their round. Brief them: "Walk around, ask questions, no judgment delivered out loud."

Watch for

The student standing alone with no visitors after 5 min. Walk over. Bring two other students with you. Don't perform pity — ask a real question and let conversation start.
§ 05 Reckoning · 5 Big Ideas pass-through 15 MIN · SOLO · TO VAULT
+65WRITE — five lines, one per Big Idea

Do

  • Slides 9–10: surface the 5 Big Ideas. Read each aloud.
  • Solo writing: one line per Big Idea, in Reflections/W08-reckoning.md. Each line references at least one note in their own vault by name (e.g., "[[Bugs/W05-bug-report]]").
  • Inside this section: re-record the W6 trust dial. The number probably moved. Why? One sentence in the AI Big Idea line.

Watch for

The student writing generic praise ("this class was great"). The reckoning is not a Yelp review. Redirect: "What specifically lives in your vault that wasn't there in W1?" Real lines reference real artifacts.
§ 06 Trajectory · what comes after 10 MIN · ONE PARAGRAPH · OPTIONAL POST
+80SHIP — what's the next thing

Do

  • One paragraph: what's the next thing you're going to ship, and what habit from this course will get you there?
  • Save to Reflections/W08-trajectory.md.
  • Optional but encouraged: post to the cohort Discord/Slack. The trajectory is a public commitment when shared.
  • Run the final Export-your-work .txt. Drop into Canvas as the closing artifact.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 08 · TROUBLESHOOTING + STUDENT HANDOUT

When things break.

SymptomMost likely fix
Student's v1 didn't ship — they're embarrassed The reckoning works on a non-shipped project too. Sit with them: "What did your vault learn that your code didn't?" The non-shipped project still has externalization to show.
Student's vault is mostly empty Don't shame. Ask "what would you keep from the last 7 weeks if you had to pick three things?" Write three notes now. Better late than never. Grade them on the reckoning's quality, not the vault's pre-existing depth.
Demo round 2 leaks 8 minutes Without the hard-stop signal, every round dies. If you forgot the bell, use a sharp clap and a verbal "switch" cue. The signal matters more than the device.
One student is hogging the round 2 table Sit at that table briefly. Direct a question at the quietest member. The shift is usually visible in 30 seconds.
Student's reckoning is generic Yelp-style praise "This class was great" is not a reckoning. Redirect: "What specifically lives in your vault that wasn't there in W1?" If they can't answer, sit with them and walk through one Big Idea slowly.
Faculty observer asks aloud "is that all you built?" Intercept gently. Round 3 is not a critique forum. Brief observers in advance — questions are welcome, judgment delivered out loud is not.

§ G Student handout · post on Canvas

A 1-page reference for after class. Suggested format below.

Final Demo + Reckoning — what you walked out with

  • v1 demo: recorded by ___ (TA) · clip stored at ___
  • 5 Big Ideas reckoning: Reflections/W08-reckoning.md · references ___ vault notes
  • Trust dial: W6 score ___ · W8 score ___ · moved because ___
  • Trajectory: the next thing I'm going to ship is ___ · the habit that will get me there is ___
  • Final export .txt: dropped into Canvas at ___

REMEMBERThe vault, the reckoning, and the trajectory are yours. They outlast the course. The most useful thing you can do in the next month is open the vault one more time and add a note about how W8 felt — because the gap between "what I thought I'd do" and "what I actually did" is the data your future self will need.
