PURPOSE

Why this exists

Most freshmen never ship anything in their first year. The cost is iteration tolerance — they fear rewrites, fear ugly first drafts, fear public-facing work. v0.1 is the antidote.
TASK

What to do

In class: produce a v0.1 of your project. Code, prototype, document — any of these counts. Constraint: testable by another human in under 5 minutes. Push to GitHub. Capture two classmate observations in your vault.
CRITERIA

How you're scored

Four criteria, scored Exemplary / Proficient / Developing / Not yet. Total 60 pts. Working > polished. Public > private.

DELIVERABLE 2 · RECKONING

The reckoning.

40 PTS40
PURPOSE

Why this exists

Shipping without reflecting is a one-time event. Reflecting after every ship is the habit that makes iteration cumulative — what you learned in v0.1 informs v0.2, v0.3, v1. The reckoning is where the lesson sticks.
TASK

What to do

In Reflections/W04-reckoning.md, write three sentences. Under 60 words total. What shipped. What didn't. What next week needs. Bullet points OK; padding not OK.
CRITERIA

How you're scored

Three criteria, scored Exemplary / Proficient / Developing / Not yet. Total 40 pts. Specificity is the load-bearing trait — vague reckonings score below specific ones, even if the project shipped well.

Rubric — three criteria · four levels

Criterion Exemplary Proficient Developing Not yet PTS
"What shipped" is specific15 pts 15 / 15Names the actual artifact + a quality attribute. "v0.1 landing page renders in Chrome but breaks on Safari." 12 / 15Names the artifact: "v0.1 landing page works." 8 / 15Generic. "Shipped something." 0 / 15Missing or copied from the rubric. / 15
"What didn't" is honest15 pts 15 / 15Names a specific gap with a real reason. "Save button doesn't persist — I didn't know how to set up local storage and I didn't push through." 12 / 15Names a gap. Reason missing or generic. 8 / 15Vague: "Some things didn't work." 0 / 15"Everything worked" or missing. / 15
