CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 04 · WHY THIS LESSON

Why now, why this way.

Most freshmen in CS never ship. They take notes, pass quizzes, write small assignment scripts, and arrive at sophomore year having never produced a thing someone else could touch. The cost is iteration tolerance — they fear rewrites, fear the first ugly draft, and fear public-facing work for the next four years.

Project Seed v0.1 closes the spawn-to-ship loop in a single class. Re-read the W1 seed, carve a v0.1 (the smallest, ugliest version that ships), build it for 35 minutes with help in the room, get one classmate observation, and write a three-sentence reckoning. The version is not impressive. The fact that it exists is the lesson.

§ A Outcomes — what every student leaves with

  • A v0.1 of their project. Code, prototype, document — any of these counts. Constraint: testable by another human in under 5 minutes.
  • Two classmate observations captured in their vault (Bugs/ folder).
  • A three-sentence reckoning in Reflections/ — what shipped, what didn't, what next week needs.
  • At least one git commit dated today. The history is the proof of iteration.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 04 · PRE-CLASS · 24 HOURS OUT

Pre-class checklist.

Project Seed v0.1 fails when the build block leaks or when seeds are missing. Run the checklist 24 hours before class.

§ C Room + tech

  • Confirm the room can be re-arranged. Studio-style means desks together in pods of 3–4. Lecture rows kill the energy.
  • Power at every seat. Backup strip in the bag.
  • Confirm Wi-Fi handles 20+ pushes to GitHub at once. Setup Quest verified working stacks; W4 is when GitHub gets a real workout.
  • Music. Pick a moderate-tempo instrumental playlist for § 03. Have it queued before students arrive.
  • If projecting a timer, queue a 35-minute countdown with audible 5-min and 2-min warnings.
  • Creator Commons booking for any student whose v0.1 needs physical making (laser cut, 3D print, etc.). Coordinate this in advance.

§ D Seed + setup integrity

  • Run a Canvas check: does every student have a W1 Spawn Point seed posted? If > 2 students don't, plan a 5-minute "rough seed" exercise during § 01 instead of the re-read.
  • Run a setup spot-check: does every student have a W3 GitHub repo? If a student is missing one, pair them with a TA at the start of § 03 — they cannot push without it.
  • Have W1 seed templates printed (1-pager) for any student whose seed has shifted or who never posted.

§ E Materials

  • Slides + speaker notes (`project-seed-v01-slides.html`)
  • TILT rubric (`project-seed-v01-tilt-rubric.html`)
  • v0.1 carving worksheet (1-pager, printed — backup if vaults are flaky)
  • Cardboard, scissors, hot-glue, sticky notes (for non-code v0.1s)
  • Music + queued 35-min timer
  • Two-color sticky notes for the public test (yellow = observation, pink = question)

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 04 · RUN OF SHOW · §§ 00–02

Run of show. First half.

§ 00 Cold open · the gap 5 MIN · LAPTOPS CLOSED · LAUNCH ENERGY
+0OPEN — the gap is the lesson
There is a gap between an idea and a thing someone can touch. Most of you have only ever seen the polished side of other people's work — finished apps, finished essays, finished demos. The unpolished side is invisible to you because nobody publishes it. Today, in 90 minutes, you're going to ship the unpolished side of yours. The version we make today is not impressive. The fact that it exists is the lesson.

Do

  • Slides 1–2: anchor question + Big Ideas (#1 iteration · #4 externalize).
  • One question to the room: "What's the most polished thing you've seen this week?" Pause. "How many drafts do you think it took?" Don't fill the silence.
  • Hand to TAs: "We have 35 minutes of build time. The single most important thing we can do is protect it."
§ 01 Re-read your seed 10 MIN · OBSIDIAN OPEN · MARK CHANGES
+5OPEN — vault → Project/seed

Do

  • Students open Obsidian. Find the W1 Spawn Point seed in Project/. (Anyone whose vault is missing it — a TA pairs.)
  • Read your own words from three weeks ago. Mark in a new note (Project/seed-v04-revision.md): what changed in your head since W1?
  • Three weeks ago you were before W2 Compute Time, before W3 Setup Quest. Your seed is allowed to have moved. Mark the move; don't pretend you still believe the W1 version if you don't.

Watch for

The student whose seed is "lost" or "I changed my mind so much it doesn't matter." Both flags mean: spend 90 seconds with them, get a new seed on a sticky note. The seed is allowed to be rough. The seed is not allowed to be missing.
§ 02 Carve v0.1 15 MIN · CONSTRAINT IS THE TOOL
+15SHRINK — v0.1, not v1
v0.1 is not "small for now, big later." v0.1 is the smallest, ugliest version that ships. One sticky note. One user, even if it's you. One thing it does. Constraint: another human can test it in under 5 minutes.

Do

  • Distribute the v0.1 carving worksheet (or load the template in Obsidian).
  • Three boxes: WHO (one specific person, not "users"), WHAT (one verb, one outcome), HOW SOMEONE TESTS IT (under 5 min).
  • Walk the room. The trap is "and also it could…" — every "also" makes v0.1 too big.

Watch for

Scope creep disguised as ambition. The student who says "v0.1 has to log in, then save, then…" — interrupt. "What if v0.1 just had the logged-in screen as a static page? Could you ship that in 35 minutes?" Almost always: yes. The lesson is the carve.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 04 · RUN OF SHOW · §§ 03–06

Run of show. Second half.

§ 03 Build 35 MIN · STUDIO MODE · NO LECTURE INTERRUPTION
+30BUILD — music on, timer started, TAs floating
Make a thing. Don't ask permission. If you're stuck for more than 4 minutes, raise a hand — a TA will come over. The single most important sentence today: it's OK if it doesn't work. That's data.

Facilitator move

  • Start the music. Start the visible 35-min timer.
  • Float. Don't sit. If a student catches your eye, ask one question; don't solve.
  • The temptation to lecture for 5 minutes mid-build is real and wrong. Resist it. The build IS the lesson.
  • 5-min warning: visible + audible. 2-min warning: visible only.

TA moves

  • Float. Stand. The room should look like a studio, not a help desk.
  • Pair-coach: when a student asks for help, ask "what have you tried?" before doing anything else.
  • Flag stuck students to the facilitator after 4 min. Don't carry them yourself.

Watch for

The student who is reading documentation for 25 minutes and not building. "What's the smallest thing you could put on screen / on cardboard / in a doc right now? Go do that. Read after." Reading is hiding from shipping.
§ 04 First public test 10 MIN · 1:1 OBSERVATIONS · STICKY NOTES
+65SHOW — your v0.1 → one classmate

Do

  • Students pair up. Each shows v0.1 to one classmate. Classmate gives ONE observation (yellow sticky) and ONE question (pink sticky).
  • Student writes both into Bugs/ (the partner's name on each note). That's the externalization.
  • Switch. Repeat with a different partner. Total: two yellow stickies, two pink stickies in Bugs/.

Watch for

The student whose v0.1 is so unfinished they're embarrassed to show. Sit with them, look at it for 30 seconds, give a real observation. The model is: this version exists, it's allowed to be ugly, here's what I noticed. They get to see what feedback is supposed to look like.
§ 05 Reckon 10 MIN · THREE SENTENCES · TO VAULT
+75WRITE — Reflections/W04-reckoning.md

Do

  • Three sentences in Reflections/. What shipped. What didn't. What next week needs.
  • Constraint: under 60 words total. Bullet points OK. The reckoning IS the lesson — most students discover that "shipping" looked smaller than they thought.
§ 06 Take-home 5 MIN · PUSH + LOG
+85SHIP — git commit, push, log
  • Push v0.1 to GitHub. The commit message names what shipped (e.g., "v0.1 — landing page only").
  • Log: GitHub URL · classmate observation count · reckoning path. Three lines in Start Log.
  • Spot-checked at 1:1s. The commit history is the proof.

CSCI-Y100 · WEEK 04 · TROUBLESHOOTING + STUDENT HANDOUT

When things break.

SymptomMost likely fix
Student has no W1 seed in their vault Hand them the printed 1-pager. Five minutes. Use the Canvas Discussion as backup if the vault is missing entirely.
"My idea is too big to ship in 35 minutes" It is. That's the lesson. Sit with them for 90 seconds: "What's the static screenshot version? The cardboard version? The one-paragraph README?" Pick the smallest. Build that.
Student is reading docs, not building Time-box: "5 more minutes of reading, then put something on screen even if it's wrong." Reading without shipping is a form of avoidance — name it.
GitHub push rejects auth (PAT expired, etc.) SSH key path from W3 should still work. Otherwise: regenerate PAT (5 min). If totally stuck, save locally + push from office hours — don't let it eat the build block.
Student wants to show v0.1 but it crashed The crash is the v0.1. Show the partner what you wrote, where it crashes, what error message says. That's a real test — not a fake one.
Reckoning is two words: "shipped, kinda" That's not three sentences. Sit with them. Ask: "What specifically shipped? What's specifically incomplete? What's the one move you'd make in W5?" Write it as they speak.

§ F Student handout · post on Canvas

A 1-page reference for after class. Suggested format below.

v0.1 — what you actually shipped

  • v0.1 lives at: [GitHub URL] OR [physical artifact location]
  • Tested by: ___ (classmate name) · One observation: ___ · One question: ___
  • Tested by: ___ (classmate name) · One observation: ___ · One question: ___
  • What shipped: ___ · What didn't: ___ · What W5 needs: ___
