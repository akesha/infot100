③ EQUIPMENT SCAVENGER · 20 MIN · DELIVERABLE 1

Eight tools.
Eight concrete ideas.

  1. For each of 8 tools
    Write what YOU'd make with it for your final project.
  2. Specific — not "something cool"
    Real object, real user, real problem. "A phone stand for left-handed users with shaky hands" beats "a phone stand."
  3. Rate each: must / maybe / no
    Would you actually use this in 14 weeks? The "must-haves" become your project's spine.
  4. Auto-saves as you type
    You'll bring this to Week 4. Don't lose it.

07 / 14
④ CARDBOARD SPRINT · 30 MIN · DELIVERABLE 2

Build something today.

  1. Pick a problem · 3 min
    One real, small annoyance from this week. Tiny is fine. Specific is better.
  2. Sketch · 5 min
    On paper, or in Tinkercad if it'll help you see it in 3D. Don't perfect it — commit a shape.
  3. Build · 18 min
    Cardboard. Tape. Hot glue. ChompSaw for curves. Cameo for precision. Don't make it pretty. Make it WORK.
  4. Photo + reflect · 4 min
    Photograph it. Three lines: what worked, what broke, what you'd change.

↳ THE LESSON BEHIND THE ACTIVITY

Cardboard is the
world's fastest 3D printer.

90 seconds per iteration — not six hours. Senior designers prototype in cardboard their entire careers because the loop is faster than thinking about it.
The thing you just built is small. The skill you just used — pick a problem · commit a shape · make a thing · notice what broke — is the entire course in miniature. You did it in 30 minutes. You'll do it for 14 weeks.
09 / 14
⑤ PROJECT SEED · 10 MIN · DELIVERABLE 3

14 weeks from now:
what ships?

  1. Who is it for?
    A specific person. "Freshmen in Briscoe" beats "students."
  2. What's their problem?
    The pain — not the solution. What annoys, breaks, or wastes their time?
  3. What will you make?
    A physical thing. Be concrete: a stand, a kit, a wearable, an enclosure.
  4. Which 2–3 verbs combined?
    From the eight: cut · print · stitch · press · cast · protect · capture · design.
  5. Smallest version by Week 4?
    Ugly v0.1 a real user could touch. Not finished — testable.
