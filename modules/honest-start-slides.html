② THE 4 TRIGGERS · 8 MIN · ANCHOR CONCEPT

Same surface.
Different fuel.

AMBIGUITY
"I don't know what 'done' looks like."
FIX: Make it tiny & concrete. Define done for the next 25 min only. Worst-possible first paragraph.
FEAR
"Starting will confirm something I'm scared of."
FIX: Lower the stakes of step one. Open the doc; don't write. Read the rubric; don't draft. Talk to one human.
BOREDOM
"My brain refuses to engage with this."
FIX: Add structure or stakes. Pomodoro. Body-double with a friend. Connect it to something you actually care about.
EXHAUSTION
"I'm running on fumes and pretending I'm not."
FIX: Stop. Eat, hydrate, walk, nap. Then one tiny start. The fix that feels least productive is the most.
Two triggers can stack. Diagnose the biggest one first — the others will surface.

④ PROCRASTINATION AUTOPSY · 25 MIN · DELIVERABLE #1

Diagnose one moment.

  1. Locate it in time
    When · where · what was due · what you did instead.
  2. Find the trigger
    Ambiguity? Fear? Boredom? Exhaustion? Match it to section 02.
  3. Find what's underneath
    What were you protecting yourself from? One honest sentence.
  4. Prescribe a tiny step
    Something small enough you'll do it tonight. Halve it. Halve it again.

⑤ MOTIVATION STACK · 20 MIN · DELIVERABLE #2

Build a ritual that
survives a bad week.

  1. Three autonomy levers
    Where can you choose? Time of day, location, music, order.
  2. Three mastery levers
    What does progress look like? Concrete signal — not vibes.
  3. Three purpose levers
    Why does this matter to a human you love — present-you, future-you, someone?
  4. One 25-min ritual
    When/where/what + which of your nine levers it pulls. Run it tonight.
