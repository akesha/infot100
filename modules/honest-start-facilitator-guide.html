PAGE 2 · WHY & WHEN

§ 01Why this module exists

Procrastination is the single most common reason first-years tank a course they're capable of acing. The standard advice — "just start," "use a planner," "Pomodoro" — fails because it treats all procrastination as one thing. It isn't. Avoiding a paper because you don't have a thesis is not the same problem as avoiding it because you're terrified of being judged. The fixes are different. Conflating them is why students keep failing the same advice.

This module separates the four triggers, gives students a forensic tool to diagnose their own moment, and pairs the diagnosis with the only universal cure: a deliberately bad first attempt. They leave with two artifacts and an accountability partner.

THE ANCHOR CONCEPT Procrastination is data. The trigger tells you the fix. Ambiguity → make it tiny. Fear → lower the stakes of step one. Boredom → add structure or stakes. Exhaustion → stop and rest. Same surface, different fuel.

§ 02Outcomes

By the end of class, students will be able to:

  • Name the four triggers in their own words and recognize each one in a real example.
  • Diagnose a specific recent moment of avoidance — not generalize ("I procrastinate") but locate it in time and underneath.
  • Practice the bad-first-attempt technique on a real assignment, in 90 seconds, in class.
  • Identify their personal autonomy / mastery / purpose levers and design one 25-min ritual that uses all three.
  • Commit to a tiny step due tonight, with a partner who will text them.

PAGE 3 · PREP & OPENING

§ 04Pre-class checklist (24 hrs out)

  • Open the module page on the projector — confirm internet, screen, audio.
  • Have one personal procrastination moment ready — yours, recent, specific.
  • Print 1× Pair-Up handout per student (page 6 of this guide).
  • Whiteboard markers ready — you'll do a quick tally during 00.
  • Post the slide deck link in Canvas.
  • Glance at last week's reflections — note any students who flagged "stuck."
  • Glass of water within reach. The room runs warm during the Autopsy.
  • Confirm the speaker notes show in your presenter view.
§ 05
00 · Avoidance Wall
10 MIN · 00:00 — 00:10 · SLIDES 02–03
PHASE 1 0:00 — 0:04  ·  SETUP + TALLY

There's a thing on your to-do list right now that you're avoiding. I know there is. You know there is. We're going to put it on the wall — anonymously — and watch the wall fill up.

  • Show the live tally on the projector. Walk through the 10 categories aloud.
  • Tell students: "Pick the one that fits best. One click. The bars move."
  • Wait until everyone has clicked. Don't rush. Let the silence do work.
PHASE 2 0:04 — 0:10  ·  LET IT LAND
  • Read the top three categories aloud. Don't editorialize.
  • Ask: "Anyone surprised by what's on top?" Take 1–2 reactions.
  • Ask: "Anyone here clicked something and was relieved to see other people clicked it too?"
  • Land on the takeaway box.

You're not weird. You're not lazy. You're avoiding something specific, for a specific reason. Today we name the reason — because the reason tells you the fix.

WATCH FOR · DON'T MORALIZE

If a student tries to confess publicly ("I haven't started anything in 3 weeks"), affirm it briefly — "Good. That's data. We'll work with it." — and move on. This is not the moment for advice.

§ 06
01 · Cold open
5 MIN · 00:10 — 00:15 · SLIDE 04
FULL 5 MIN 0:10 — 0:15
  • Click through Maya, then Devon, then Priya. Read each card aloud — including "what's underneath."
  • Pause after each: "Show of hands — who's been Maya / Devon / Priya?"
  • End with the handoff: same surface, different fuel.

If you call all three of these students "lazy," you'll prescribe the wrong fix to two of them. The fix has to match the fuel.

PAGE 4 · ANCHOR + DRILL

§ 07
02 · The 4 Triggers
8 MIN · 00:15 — 00:23 · SLIDE 05
ANCHOR 0:15 — 0:23  ·  HOLD THIS SLIDE

This is the spine of the whole module. Don't rush it. Hold the slide for the full 8 minutes.

DO THIS

  • Walk through each trigger — about 90 seconds each.
  • For each, ask: "What does this sound like in your head when it's happening?" Take 1 student answer.
  • Then read the prescribed fix. Notice it's different every time.
  • End on the "two triggers stacked" tape strip.

SAY THIS

AMBIGUITY: I don't know what 'done' looks like. Fix: make it tiny.

FEAR: starting will confirm something I'm scared of. Fix: lower the stakes of step one.

BOREDOM: my brain refuses to engage. Fix: add structure or stakes.

EXHAUSTION: I'm running on fumes. Fix: stop and rest. Then a tiny start.

KEY PHRASE TO WRITE ON THE BOARD

"Procrastination is data — the trigger tells you the fix." This sentence will do more lifting than any other in the module.

§ 08
03 · Start Bad on Purpose
12 MIN · 00:23 — 00:35 · SLIDE 06 · LIVE DRILL
LIVE 0:23 — 0:35  ·  ALL STUDENTS WRITING
  • Read the 4 rules aloud. Linger on rule 4 — nobody will see this.
  • Have everyone open the in-page tool. Type a real assignment they're avoiding.
  • Hit start together. 90 seconds. You write too — model it.
  • When time hits zero, ask: "How many words did you have 90 seconds ago? How many do you have now?"
  • Ask 1–2 students to share not their writing — only the word count and how it felt.

Motivation comes after the first sentence. You've been told it's the other way around. It isn't. The blank page is the boss fight, and you just beat it.

IF A STUDENT FREEZES

Some students will sit and not type. Don't pressure. Whisper one prompt: "What's the worst possible first sentence?" — that often unlocks. If still nothing, that's also data — they may be in fear/exhaustion territory; flag for the Autopsy.

↓ 0:35 — 0:37  ·  TRANSITION TO AUTOPSY · SLIDE 07

In a minute you're going to do something forensic. Pick ONE specific time in the last 7 days you avoided something. Not "I always procrastinate" — Tuesday 9:47pm, paper open, you instead reorganized your desk. That moment.

  • Walk the room while they think.
  • Anyone who can't think of a moment? They're picking too big a thing — prompt them: "what about a 5-minute thing you avoided yesterday?"

PAGE 5 · DELIVERABLES + CLOSE

§ 09
04 · Procrastination Autopsy — deliverable #1
25 MIN · 00:37 — 01:02 · SLIDE 08 · 40 PTS
QUIET WORK 0:37 — 1:02  ·  WALK THE ROOM

SETUP (FIRST 3 MIN)

  • Read the brief aloud — emphasize "specific moment, not general pattern."
  • Confirm: "Saves to your browser as you go. You will not lose your work."
  • Show the trigger picker — they'll need section 02's framework.

WHILE THEY WORK (22 MIN)

  • Walk a slow loop. Stay quiet. This is hard work.
  • If a student looks teary — a real autopsy can sting. Whisper "this is the right work; take a breath."
  • If a student finishes early, prompt: "What's the second moment? Run another."
  • At 0:55 — call the 7-minute warning.

COMMON STUCK POINTS

"All four triggers apply." → Pick the biggest. The others will surface later. "My tiny step still feels too big." → Halve it. Then halve it again. "Open the document" is a complete tiny step.

§ 10
05 · Motivation Stack — deliverable #2
20 MIN · 01:02 — 01:22 · SLIDE 09 · 40 PTS
QUIET WORK 1:02 — 1:22  ·  STILL WALKING
  • Read aloud: autonomy, mastery, purpose. Use the example seeds on each card.
  • First 8 min: students fill in their own three levers per category (9 total).
  • Next 10 min: design ONE 25-min ritual. When/where/what + how it hits each lever.
  • Last 2 min: ask 2–3 students to read their ritual aloud. Don't critique — celebrate concreteness.

THE LEVER STUDENTS DODGE

PURPOSE is the axis students half-ass. Push it: "'Because it's on the syllabus' doesn't count. Why does this matter to a person — present-you, future-you, someone you love?" If a student can't articulate purpose, the ritual won't survive a bad week.

§ 11
06 · Pair-up + take-home + closer
8 MIN · 01:22 — 01:30 · SLIDES 10–13
CLOSE 1:22 — 1:30
  • Hand out the Pair-Up sheet (page 6). Have students pair — preferably across majors.
  • Each shares only their tiny step due tonight + their phone number.
  • Show the Start Log template. Confirm it's pinned in Canvas.
  • Closer slide. Read it aloud. Don't soften it.
  • Q&A — 2–3 minutes. Stay open.

Feeling better isn't the goal. Doing the thing is. You don't have to feel motivated tonight — you have to text "DONE" to your partner. That's the whole assignment.

COMMON Q&A — BE READY

QuestionHonest answer
"What if my partner ghosts me?"Then you ping them. The protocol works in either direction. Email me if it dies.
"What if I can't pick a single trigger?"Pick the biggest. The others will show up in your Start Log within 3 days.
"Is this just CBT?"It borrows from CBT, behavioral activation, and self-determination theory. None of which are magic. All of which beat 'just try harder.'
"What if my ritual doesn't work?"Good — that's data too. The Start Log will tell you which lever was missing. Redesign on Sunday.

PAGE 6 · STUDENT HANDOUT — PRINT 1 PER STUDENT

Pair-Up. 7 days.

Find one person — preferably not in your major. Trade phone numbers. Run the protocol below for one week. Bring your Start Log to next class.

YOUR NAME

PARTNER'S NAME

PARTNER'S PHONE

PARTNER'S MAJOR

YOUR TINY STEP, DUE TONIGHT

e.g. "By 10pm tonight: open the Y395 doc and write 3 bad sentences."

YOUR PARTNER'S TINY STEP

AMBIGUITY
"I don't know what 'done' looks like."
FIX: Make it tiny & concrete. Define done for the next 25 min only. Worst-possible first paragraph.
FEAR
"Starting will confirm something I'm scared of."
FIX: Lower the stakes of step one. Open the doc; don't write. Read the rubric; don't draft. Talk to one human.
BOREDOM
"My brain refuses to engage with this."
FIX: Add structure or stakes. Pomodoro. Body-double with a friend. Connect to something you actually care about.
EXHAUSTION
"I'm running on fumes and pretending I'm not."
FIX: Stop. Eat, hydrate, walk, nap. Then one tiny start. The fix that feels least productive is the most.

THE PROTOCOL · ONE WEEK

  1. Tonight: text your partner "DONE" when you complete your tiny step. No essay. No excuse.
  2. Don't text by tomorrow noon? Your partner pings you.
  3. Each day: log one Start Moment in your Start Log (template in Canvas).
  4. Sunday: text your partner ONE thing you noticed about your patterns this week.
  5. Bring the log to next class. We'll read patterns together.

The pair-up is the strategy. Your motivation is what shows up because someone is waiting on a text — not before.
